## Every Fee That Gets Discounted

Let's be precise here, because GMX has several different fee types and the referral discount does not apply to all of them. Knowing which fees are affected helps you understand exactly where your savings land.

### Fees the PRO code discounts:

**Position fees (open/close)** — This is the big one. Every time you open or close a leveraged position, GMX charges a fee based on the notional position size:
- Standard pairs: approximately 0.07% to open, 0.07% to close
- Select GM pool pairs: as low as 0.05% on some assets in GMX V2

The PRO code takes 10% off these fees. On a $50,000 position, that's a saving of $3.50 to $7.00 per round trip. Multiply that across a week of active trading and you're saving meaningful amounts of real money.

**Swap fees** — When you use GMX's swap interface (not perps), a position fee still applies. The referral discount also reduces this fee component.

### Fees the PRO code does NOT affect:

**Borrow fees (funding)** — GMX charges an ongoing hourly borrow fee for holding leveraged positions. This is a dynamic rate based on pool utilization and is separate from the execution fee. Referral codes have no impact on borrow rates.

**Gas fees (execution fees)** — Every trade on-chain requires a gas payment. These go to the Arbitrum or Avalanche network, not to GMX, so they fall outside the referral system entirely.

**Price impact** — Large trades on low-liquidity pairs can incur price impact. This is a market structure effect, not a protocol fee, so the discount doesn't touch it.

Understanding this distinction matters for your P&L modeling. The 10% applies to the explicit position fees — the most consistent and predictable cost of trading on GMX.

## GMX V2: What Changed

GMX V2 represents a substantial architectural evolution from V1. If you've been dormant for a while or are coming from the original GMX experience, here's what you need to know.

**GM Pools replace GLP**

V1 had a single unified liquidity pool (GLP) backing all trades. V2 introduced isolated GM (GMX Market) pools — each trading pair has its own dedicated liquidity pool. This means a blow-up in one market doesn't socialize losses across the whole protocol. For LPs it's a different risk profile; for traders it means more granular liquidity conditions per asset.

**Synthetic assets and expanded pairs**

V2 added support for synthetic markets — you can now trade assets like DOGE, LTC, SOL, BNB, and others without those assets needing to be directly held in a pool. This dramatically expanded the available trading pairs beyond the original ETH/BTC/AVAX/UNI/LINK lineup.

**Tiered fee structure**

Position fees in V2 are slightly more dynamic. Some pairs have lower fees (closer to 0.05%) while others sit at the traditional 0.07%. The referral discount applies uniformly regardless of which pair you're trading, so PRO gives you 10% off whatever the base rate is for that market.

**Oracle-based price feeds with keepers**

V2 refined the oracle and keeper mechanism for order execution. Stop-losses, take-profits, and limit orders are handled by keeper bots rather than pure on-chain automation, which improves execution reliability while maintaining the non-custodial model.

**Higher leverage options**

V2 supports leverage up to 100x on select markets, up from 30x in V1. Combined with lower fees on some pairs, it makes the platform genuinely competitive with centralized perp exchanges for certain trading styles.

## Arbitrum vs Avalanche

GMX operates on both Arbitrum (Layer 2 on Ethereum) and Avalanche (its own L1). Both support the full GMX experience including the PRO referral code, but there are meaningful practical differences.

### Arbitrum

**Liquidity:** Significantly deeper. The majority of GMX's total value locked sits on Arbitrum, which means tighter spreads, less price impact on larger trades, and more reliable fills on limit orders.

**Gas fees:** Arbitrum's L2 compression keeps gas costs low — typically a few cents to a dollar per transaction depending on Ethereum mainnet conditions. Extremely cheap relative to Ethereum mainnet.

**Token ecosystem:** Arbitrum has the richer DeFi ecosystem around it. If you're also using Uniswap, Aave, Camelot, or other protocols, keeping funds on Arbitrum reduces bridging friction.

**Best for:** Most traders. If you're not sure which chain to use, start on Arbitrum.

### Avalanche

**Liquidity:** Smaller but still substantial. Avalanche liquidity on GMX has grown considerably since launch, and for standard ETH/BTC/AVAX pairs the depth is adequate for most position sizes.

**Gas fees:** AVAX-denominated gas. During network congestion AVAX gas can spike more than Arbitrum's compressed fees, though normal conditions are very cheap.

**Speed:** Avalanche's 1-second finality is extremely fast. Position confirmations feel nearly instant on a good connection.

**Token ecosystem:** Makes sense if you're already active in the Avalanche DeFi ecosystem (Trader Joe, Benqi, etc.) and prefer not to bridge to Arbitrum.

**Best for:** Avalanche-native traders, AVAX holders, anyone already running on that ecosystem.

**Remember:** You need to set the PRO referral code separately on each chain if you want the discount on both networks.

## Earning With GMX

Beyond trading, GMX offers two primary yield mechanisms for participants who want protocol-side exposure.

### Staking GMX

The GMX token is the protocol's governance and fee-sharing token. Staking GMX earns:

- **ETH/AVAX rewards** from 30% of protocol fee revenue distributed to stakers
- **esGMX (escrowed GMX)** — additional token rewards that vest over 12 months or can be staked themselves for compounding returns
- **Multiplier Points** — earned at 100% APR just for being staked, which boost your ETH yield percentage over time

Multiplier Points are a loyalty mechanism: the longer you stake without unstaking, the higher your fee-sharing multiplier becomes. Unstaking resets your Multiplier Points proportionally.

### Providing GM Liquidity

In GMX V2, you can deposit into individual GM pools as a liquidity provider. GMs take the other side of traders — when traders lose, GMs win; when traders profit, GMs pay out. Over longer time horizons with a diverse set of traders on both sides, GM pools have historically generated positive returns in addition to fee income.

GMs earn a share of:
- Position open/close fees
- Borrow fees from leveraged positions
- Swap fees through the pool

Unlike GLP in V1, GM pools are isolated — you're only exposed to the specific market you LP into, not the entire platform's risk. This lets you pick the markets you're most comfortable with.
